Tips for Accuracy

  • Use a well-lit environment with a plain background
  • Keep your hand centered in the camera frame
  • Hold each sign steady for 1 second until the bar fills
  • Use Space for spaces, Backspace to delete
  • Press M to switch between Letters and Numbers
  • Click "✓ Done" to commit a word to your sentence
💡 Practice each letter slowly before speeding up for best results.
Our Mission

About SignBridge

"Communication is a human right.
Technology should make it accessible to everyone."

SignBridge was created with a single purpose: to make sign language accessible, learnable, and usable by anyone with a web browser. No downloads. No installations. Just open and sign.

We believe learning should be fun, interactive, and empowering. SignBridge turns sign language education into an engaging experience — learn at your own pace, practice with real-time AI feedback, and build confidence one sign at a time. Whether you're a student, educator, or simply curious, this platform makes ASL accessible to everyone in their own way.

Built on American Sign Language (ASL), the most widely used sign language in North America, SignBridge uses cutting-edge AI to recognize hand shapes in real-time through your webcam.

Over 430 million people worldwide live with disabling hearing loss, and an estimated 72 million are profoundly deaf — relying on sign language as their primary means of communication. Beyond the deaf community, more than 70 million people globally are non-speaking due to conditions such as autism, cerebral palsy, aphasia, or other speech-related disabilities, many of whom also turn to sign language as a vital bridge to the world. By lowering the barrier to learning ASL, we hope to foster a more inclusive world where communication knows no boundaries.
